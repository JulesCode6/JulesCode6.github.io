Adventures

Arches

I took our children to Arches once, it was me alone with our four children. My husband was on a work trip and so I decided we should go on an adventure also. We set out, drove into the park and went to some of the popular stops. We hiked to balance rock and posed on one foot by it, doing our own balancing act. Each of us taking our turn. The children almost ran the whole way. (It is a very short one, about half a mile). During this hike some dark ominous clouds rolled in and I knew our time was limited.

We drove on to our next destination of Double Arch. The clouds were coming in fast but this was another short hike. We had some jackets but were not prepared for rain. About half way to the Arches the heavens opened up and a downpour ensued, and included with it were little hail pellets. It was glorious, and also something we needed to get out of since we were not prepared with the right gear. We started to run back to the car and at several points I was carrying two children in my arms and trying to protect them from being pelted by the hail. It was an incredible moment of both majesty from the elements and panic at my little ones needing to be protected. Rushing back up there were sandy steps that had become a small river we had to wade through to get back to the car. Make it back we did though. Everyone was a little harried from the experience but also awed by it.

We were lucky enough to make it back in the park a day or two later to get back on the bike so to say for my littlest ones who did not enjoy the experience.

Adventuring with children can be unpredictable and being prepared for every challenge that might arise is difficult. However I have found that even through some of our most challenging experiences we have found ways to enjoy them. And our children keep wanting to do them. Maybe it is luck that they are willing to. But maybe it is keeping a good attitude, or having appropriate gear, or maybe just the majesty of the great outdoors that beckons them back for more. Or perhaps it is some combination of it all.

Adventure on!
